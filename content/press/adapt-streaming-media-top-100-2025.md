*December 15, 2025 – Denver, CO*

Adapt Global Studios has been recognized in Streaming Media's 2025 Top 100, a list highlighting the most innovative and influential companies shaping the streaming media industry. Adapt is featured among the 100 firms "driving the evolution of content delivery and streaming technology," alongside leading global companies including The Walt Disney Company and Apple.

The Top 100 list, curated by Streaming Media Magazine, celebrates companies that are "pushing the boundaries of streaming innovation, accessibility, and audience engagement." Adapt stood out for its Nuance platform, a cloud-native ecosystem that integrates transcription, translation, voice generation, and lip-sync dubbing — powered by a trained global workforce of Cultural Ambassadors.

> "We are honored to be included in Streaming Media's Top 100," said Justin Beaudin, Founder and CEO of Adapt. "It's a recognition of the great work both our technology team and our global linguistics team are doing to push the boundaries of what's possible in media localization."

## The Nuance Platform: What Earned the Recognition

At the heart of Adapt's inclusion is the Nuance platform — a cloud-native ecosystem purpose-built for high-volume, high-fidelity localization. Nuance integrates AI tools for every stage of the workflow: automated speech recognition (ASR) with regional dialect modeling, neural machine translation via DeepL, voice synthesis through ElevenLabs, and lip-sync alignment with Adapt's proprietary RealSync technology. These AI systems are orchestrated within a centralized order management environment that connects project managers, Cultural Ambassadors, and Tech Producers across time zones and languages.

What distinguishes Adapt from general-purpose AI translation tools is the human layer. Each localized output is reviewed, adjusted, and approved by Cultural Ambassadors — regionally native professionals with backgrounds in film dubbing, broadcast journalism, and voice performance. This human-in-the-loop model is not simply a QC step; it is embedded throughout the workflow, ensuring that cultural nuance, comedic timing, emotional arc, and speaker personality are preserved in every target language.

## Why Streaming Media Inclusion Matters

Streaming Media Magazine's Top 100 is notable for its breadth — it spans the full streaming value chain, from content delivery networks and cloud infrastructure to content production tools and audience analytics. Being recognized in the same list as major studios and technology platforms signals that localization is increasingly understood as a core component of the streaming product experience, not an afterthought.

For studios and broadcasters evaluating localization partners, third-party recognition like this provides a signal of credibility and market validation. For Adapt, it supports conversations with enterprise clients and investors who use industry lists as part of their vendor diligence process.
