*February 13, 2026 – Denver, CO*

Adapt, a next generation localization company, has engaged human linguists to deliver high quality AI-enabled multilingual dubs for Flashback Pictures' vertical microdrama *Pizza Guys vs Vampires*.

Adapt's model integrates advanced AI tools for transcription, translation, and voice generation with a curated network of native human linguists. This human-in-the-loop approach ensures that tone, humor, pacing, and emotional nuance are preserved so stories resonate authentically in each target language.

"This collaboration demonstrates how AI, when paired with expert linguists, can unlock global distribution opportunities for emerging content formats like microdrama."

> "Today, global accessibility is essential for any content creator. Responsible implementation of AI requires responsible participation." — Mariko Nomura, Producer, Flashback Pictures

## How the Localization Was Produced

Adapt's workflow for *Pizza Guys vs Vampires* followed the company's standard human-in-the-loop model. After receiving the source files, Adapt's AI systems generated an initial transcription, followed by machine translation into each target language. Cultural Ambassadors — regionally native professionals with backgrounds in dubbing direction and script adaptation — then reviewed and adapted each translated script for spoken cadence, humor preservation, and emotional timing.

Voice generation was handled through Adapt's AI synthesis pipeline, with Tech Producers refining the output to match performance characteristics of the original cast. Final mixes were delivered in the target formats required for each distribution platform.

The vertical microdrama format presents specific localization challenges. Episodes are typically 60–90 seconds long, which means every word and every pause carries outsized weight. Humor and tension are compressed into moments that require precise cultural equivalents, not word-for-word translation. Adapt's Cultural Ambassador model is designed specifically for this kind of creative fidelity.

## The Broader Market for Independent Content Localization

*Pizza Guys vs Vampires* represents a growing category: independent productions with genuine commercial potential but limited budgets for traditional localization, which can run $2,000–$5,000 per minute of content per language using conventional dubbing studios. Adapt's AI-assisted workflow brings that cost down significantly, opening global distribution for content that would previously have remained English-only.

Flashback Pictures' success — a Latin America license deal from a fast-turnaround feature — demonstrates the return-on-investment case for early-stage localization investment. For content with proven audience appeal in one market, AI-enabled localization into two or three additional languages can unlock licensing revenue that far exceeds the localization cost.
